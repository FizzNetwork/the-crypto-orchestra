Why the chord filter matters so much
The raw BTC crossing signal fires 62 times in 5 years — but more than half of those are during DOMINANT (BTC positive, SOL negative) or TRITONE (both negative) chord states. Those environments are structurally hostile for SOL longs — you're fighting the chord. Removing them (S1) doesn't just trim bad trades — it reveals that the underlying signal structure is sound. The problem was always chord contamination, not the crossing signal itself.

Predicted factors for SUSPENDED → MAJOR vs TRITONE
SUSPENDED = BTC negative, SOL positive. Resolution to MAJOR requires BTC to recover. Resolution to TRITONE means SOL also falls. Factors that likely predict resolution direction (untested): (1) Macro context — bull regime SUSPENDED resolves to MAJOR, bear regime to TRITONE. (2) Magnitude of SOL's positive return at entry — strongly positive SOL is more likely to hold while BTC recovers. (3) BTC's trend leading into SUSPENDED — a brief dip in a bull trend vs a structural breakdown. (4) Macro convergence — are Gold/S&P also near their Poincaré crossings? If yes, broader risk-on = MAJOR resolution likely. This is the next research arc.

What the lunar data says
BTC Poincaré crossings don't happen more or less frequently around new or full moons — the frequency is uniform (χ²=6.39, p=0.47). But the QUALITY of signal varies by phase. New moon entries and 3rd quarter entries average +3.5–3.8% with 75–80% win rates. Waning gibbous and 1st quarter average negative. This pattern is consistent with the "lunar band" theory: assets oscillate in sync with the moon, so certain phases are naturally aligned with momentum recovery, while others coincide with resistance.
Small sample caveat
The lunar phase bins have 3–12 entries each. With n=51 total S1 trades spread across 8 bins, no single bin has enough data to be statistically conclusive. The pattern is directionally interesting — especially the consistency of new moon (phase ≈0) being strong across multiple bin analyses. But this requires a much longer data history to confirm. The right use of this finding is as a tertiary filter: all else equal, prefer entries near new moon and avoid entries near waning gibbous.

The Signal in Plain English

Every week, check two things: Is BTC's weekly return negative? Is SOL's weekly return positive? If both are true simultaneously — that's the SUSPENDED chord. Enter a SOL long position at the open of the following week.

Exit when either condition flips: if BTC goes positive (chord becomes MAJOR — best outcome, typically a strong week for SOL), or if SOL goes negative (chord becomes TRITONE or DOMINANT — cut the loss).

This will fire roughly 4–5 times per year. 55% of those trades are profitable. But the wins are significantly larger than the losses (+10% avg win vs −9.5% avg loss) when you look at the MAJOR resolution trades specifically. The overall Sharpe of 1.88 is institutional-grade if it persists out of sample.
