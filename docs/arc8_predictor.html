The SUSPENDED Filter Rule

A SUSPENDED chord fires when BTC return < 0 and SOL return > 0 in the same week. Don't take every trade — use these gates:

1
SOL weekly return > +3%

SOL must show real leadership — not just barely positive. If SOL is up 0.1% it could be noise. If it's up 4%+ while BTC is red, that's a genuine divergence signal.

AND
2
SOL 4-week relative strength vs BTC < +20%

If SOL has been massively outperforming BTC for the past month, this week's SUSPENDED is a pause before both fall, not a real reversal. Wait for SOL that's been building quietly, not one that's already extended.

OR (either of the above two, or this instead)
3
BTC has been negative for 3+ consecutive weeks

Deep BTC dips create oversold conditions. When SOL finally holds up after 3+ weeks of BTC bleeding, the coast is usually clear for a real reversal. 3/3 historical cases = MAJOR.

Why BEAR regime outperforms BULL (the key paradox)

Counterintuitively, SUSPENDED chords in bear markets resolve to MAJOR 78% of the time, while bull markets only 33%. In a bull market, a one-week BTC dip is often a failed reversal — BTC just continues down, taking SOL with it. In a bear market, BTC has been punished deeply enough that when SOL shows genuine strength, it's leading a real recovery. The bear provides the oversold fuel.

Why high SOL 4-week RS is a trap

If SOL outperformed BTC by 40%+ in the past month, entering on SUSPENDED is mean-reversion risk. SOL is overextended. When BTC dips and SOL holds — but from an already inflated level — the next move is SOL catching down to BTC. The low-RS trades are the genuine base-building signals where SOL has been humble then suddenly shows life.

Important caveat: n=7 over 5 years

This rule fires roughly 1–2 times per year. 100% win rate on 7 trades is statistically impressive but not definitive. The edge is mechanistically sound (each rule has a clear causal explanation) but we need 15–20 more signals over time to lock in confidence. Use the Sharpe (9.37) as a benchmark — if future signals degrade the Sharpe below 3, revisit the rule.

At 2× position size, filtered would compound to ~260 vs unfiltered's 168.

The 2023 TRITONE cluster (4 failures)

All 4 of the TRITONE outcomes in bull regime happened in 2023. This was a unique period: post-FTX recovery where BTC led but SOL lagged, then BTC went sideways and SOL had brief spikes that quickly faded. The high-RS filter catches 2 of these (2023-07-06 and 2023-12-21). The other 2 (2023-02-16 and 2023-05-11) fail because SOL was barely positive at entry (<1.5%), which the filter also catches.

High RS + High SOL return = Danger zone

The two most deceptive skips are 2023-07-06 (SOL +14.4%) and 2023-12-21 (SOL +26.3%) — both had HUGE SOL returns at entry, suggesting the trade should work. But both had extreme prior RS (45% and 72%). This is the overextended reversal trap: a huge SOL week looks like momentum but it's the last gasp before reversion. The RS filter correctly vetos both.

The skip bucket is telling: 27% win rate, avg -0.3%

The 11 trades we skip under the filter have a 27% win rate and break even on average. They're essentially random noise dressed up as a signal. The filter is not just finding the good trades — it's genuinely separating signal from noise. The three categories of skip: (1) weak SOL entry <1% — ambiguous entry bar; (2) overextended SOL RS >20% — mean reversion risk; (3) no consec neg BTC — insufficient oversold condition.
