What Phase Measures

The spread (SOL−BTC weekly return) oscillates on a ~4-week cycle. The Hilbert transform extracts its instantaneous phase:
0° = spread zero-crossing upward — SOL just starting to lead BTC.
90° = spread at peak — SOL maximum lead.
180° = zero-crossing downward — BTC recapturing.
270° = spread at trough — BTC maximum lead.

Signal Audit — All 7 Filter Trades & Their Phase

Key Finding: Filter Signals Fire at Phase ≈ 0°

6 of 7 confirmed filter signals (100% win rate) landed within ±38° of 0° — the moment the SOL−BTC spread crosses zero upward. This is the entry point of the orbit cycle, not the midpoint. The one outlier (Dec 2023, 265°) fired mid-cycle with an unusually large SOL jump (+13.2%) that overwhelmed the phase. The Hilbert phase is a leading indicator: the filter catches the precise moment SOL begins leading, before the divergence is obvious.

Phase as a Filter Refinement

The polar histogram shows where SUSPENDED chord bars (blue) land in the 0°–360° cycle vs where filter signals (purple) land. SUSPENDED bars concentrate in the 0°–90° quadrant (spread just crossed zero and still rising) — exactly where filter signals fire. But not all SUSPENDED bars near 0° pass the filter: amplitude < 3 and RS4w context still matter. Phase ≈ 0° is a necessary but not sufficient condition. The corrected signal depth metric (closeness to 0°, not 270°) could replace or augment the current filter rule as a continuous pre-screener.
