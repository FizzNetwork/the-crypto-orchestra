Key Finding — Jupiter Synodic Cycle

Of all 10 celestial cycles tested, Jupiter's 399-day synodic cycle shows the strongest signal clustering: R = 0.685 p = 0.031 (uncorrected) 6/7 within ±60°. Filter signals tend to fire when Jupiter is at phase ~340° in its synodic cycle — which corresponds to approximately 3 weeks before Jupiter comes to opposition with Earth.

Counter-finding: All 4 lunar cycle variants (synodic, sidereal, anomalistic, draconic) return null results. The existing moon_phase feature in the model has p=0.34 — no better than random. The lunar period correlation in the BTC/SOL spread (τ=4w autocorr) appears to be a structural property of the spread, not a predictive signal for the filter.

Statistical caveat: After Bonferroni correction for 10 cycles tested simultaneously, Jupiter p = 0.32 (not significant). With only n=7 filter signals, this is a hypothesis-generating result, not a confirmed law. The effect size (R=0.685, 6/7 pattern) is large enough to track prospectively. Recommend: add jupiter_phase field to signal_check.py and test next 5–10 signals before acting on it.

What Does Jupiter Phase ~340° Mean?

Physical Interpretation

Jupiter's synodic cycle starts at opposition (closest, brightest — phase 0°). Phase 340° = 376.7 days into the cycle = 22 days before the next opposition.

Filter signals tend to fire in the late-cycle approaching-opposition window. In market terms: Jupiter opposition historically correlates with broad risk-on peaks. Pre-opposition = markets approaching risk-on climax → SOL divergence appears first (as a leading indicator).

This is speculative. The mechanism would be: macro liquidity cycles on a ~13-month cadence happen to phase-lock with Jupiter's 13.3-month synodic period.

Jupiter + Saturn Near-Conjunction

Jupiter (399d) and Saturn (378d) synodic periods differ by only 21 days. Over 5 years they drift ~105 days out of phase — enough to distinguish them statistically (and they have different mean phases: 341° vs 40°). Their near-synchrony may amplify the signal when both are in zone simultaneously.

Lunar Null — Explanation

Why No Lunar Clustering?

The existing analysis (Arc 7) found lunar phase in the 3rd quadrant (new moon region) showed mild correlation. But the Rayleigh test on all 4 lunar cycle variants returns p > 0.34 with n=7 filter signals. This is consistent with the Arc 7 finding being a feature selection artifact — the model selected moon_phase as a feature because of its correlation with the broader dataset, not because it specifically predicts filter-quality SUSPENDED bars.

The τ=4w BTC/SOL spread autocorrelation is a genuine structural finding (Law 30 — Orbit Identity), but that orbital resonance does not make the lunar calendar itself predictive for filter timing.

Brute-Force Best Period

Scanning 14–800d in 0.5d steps, best-fit period = 78.5d (R=0.900). This should NOT be acted on: with 1500 periods tested and n=7 signals, max-R distributions show this result has corrected p ≈ 0.5 — pure noise. Period optimisation on n=7 always finds something. It means nothing.
