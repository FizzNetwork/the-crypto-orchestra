Main finding: SOL's edge is specific, not universal

The same filter that gives SOL 100% win rate produces near-random results on most other assets (46–56%). ADA and DOT are actively destructive (0% win rate — these assets keep falling through SUSPENDED into TRITONE even after BTC dips deeply). ETH is the closest runner-up at 56% / +5.5% but far weaker in conviction. The edge is in the BTC→SOL causal relationship specifically — confirmed by transfer entropy in earlier work — not in a generic "crypto recovers after BTC dips" pattern.

Why SOL and not ETH?

SOL has the highest realized beta to BTC of any large-cap, meaning it overshoots BTC on both sides. When SOL holds up during a BTC dip (SUSPENDED), it's fighting its own natural beta — that divergence is a genuine signal. ETH tracks BTC more smoothly and with less lag, so SUSPENDED is less meaningful. ADA/DOT are dying assets where SUSPENDED is just a brief pause before continued collapse — no recovery bid.

What this means for the system

The orchestra framework is valid universally — every asset has chord states vs BTC. But the trading edge lives specifically in SOL×BTC SUSPENDED. The filter is not magic — it's calibrated to SOL's dynamics. Running it blindly on other pairs without re-calibrating will lose money (see ADA, DOT). ETH could be worth its own investigation with a separately tuned filter and longer lookback, but the SOL signal should remain the primary instrument.
