TLT (bonds) at 83% — the most interesting non-crypto signal

When US Treasury bonds hold up or rise while stocks fall, the market is pricing in a "flight to safety" — but one that often resolves into a coordinated recovery, not a crash. Bonds diverging upward from stocks is the classic pre-reversal signal. 5 of 6 filtered SUSPENDED bars resolved MAJOR in the following week. Low return per trade (+1.2%) because stocks are 5–10× less volatile than SOL, but structurally the same pattern.

EWJ (Japan) and EEM (Emerging Markets) at 83%/80%

International equity markets holding up while the S&P falls often signals US-specific stress (dollar, Fed policy) rather than global recession risk. When Japan and EM stay bid during a US pullback, global growth expectations are intact — so the US follows them up. Same divergence mechanism as SOL×BTC, but across geographies instead of blockchains.

Most data points of any asset. Gold miners are a leveraged inflation/real rate play. The universal pattern and the magnitude gap

The SUSPENDED filter detects genuine divergence between correlated assets across every market. The pattern is universal. But the returns scale with volatility: SOL averages +7.1% per filtered signal; TLT averages +1.2%; GDX averages +0.5%. The framework is a volatility amplifier — the edge is largest where beta is highest. SOL is the highest-beta liquid asset we've tested. That's why it dominates. In traditional markets, the signals are real but the P&L is modest unless you're levered or trading options.
